The Book

A fine art book
unlike any other

What does Aphrodite — the Greek goddess of beauty, love, and seduction — truly look like? 100 Aphrodites answers with one hundred distinct visions.

Each illustration captures the goddess in a specific encounter: in the Library of Alexandria, on a Macedonian battlefield, beside the Nile, in a Roman bathhouse. Each one is different. Each one is fleeting.

This is not a mythology handbook. It is a visual meditation — an investigation into the archetypal figure of the feminine as theorized by depth psychology, brought to life through digital painting of exceptional quality. The work explores the fundamental paradox of Aphrodite: simultaneously free and powerful, vulnerable and invincible.

Ideal for collectors and enthusiasts of figurative art, Greek mythology, Jungian archetypes, Pre-Raphaelite painting, and the classical tradition of the female figure in Western art.

The Artist

Max Valdarno

"Just as the gods created mankind, so too did mankind create the gods. In this mutual creation, we share a unique connection with the divine."

Max Valdarno (born 1972, Tuscany) is an Italian digital artist whose work centers on a single, inexhaustible subject: Aphrodite, the Greek goddess of beauty and sensuality.

Self-taught, Valdarno draws from the tradition of 19th-century European academic painting and the Pre-Raphaelite Brotherhood — artists who believed that beauty itself was a form of truth. His paintings combine the compositional rigor of classical figurative art with the expressive possibilities of digital media.

His major work, 100 Aphrodites (2023), brings together one hundred original full-page illustrations of the goddess. The project is both an artistic undertaking and an investigation into the archetypal figure of the feminine — what depth psychology, from Jung to Hillman, identifies as one of the foundational archetypes of human experience.

Valdarno lives in Friuli, northeastern Italy.
